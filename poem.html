The Betrayed, War-torn Love

- By 'Unforgiving Soul'

Love was a whisper, a shadow in flames,
A promise unspoken, now lost in the games.
Betrayal, the sword, with a kiss on its edge,
Cut through my heart, a blood-red pledge.
Your smile, once tender, now sharp as a blade,
A token of peace, but a war soon displayed.
Sadistic in nature, you tore me apart,
A battlefield forged deep within my heart.
You fought with your silence, a soldier so cold,
In the trenches of lies, your betrayal unfolds.
I, a casualty, bleeding for trust,
Yet still I returned, my heart a thin rust.
Love, a soldier with no shield or sword,
Waging its battles, though its cause is ignored.
Hope was a traitor, whispering lies,
While your love, like war, made me wear my disguise.
In the chaos, I bled, yet still, I fought,
Chasing the remnants of a love once sought.
A war within, raging, fierce, and raw,
But in your eyes, I found only flaw.
Betrayed by the one who swore to defend,
I now stand alone, a soul to amend.
The battle is endless, my spirit worn thin,
Yet I still love you, though it’s where I begin.
In the trenches of love, where nothing’s pure,
I fought for you, knowing the war would endure.
You, the enemy, my heart the prize,
And in the smoke, I saw through the lies.
Love and betrayal, intertwined in the fight,
In the war of the soul, there’s no wrong, no right.
But still, I return, to the battlefield’s glare,
For in your arms, I find despair.
